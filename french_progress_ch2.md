## 🧱 Grammar

### 44. venir de + infinitive — "to have just done"
Use present of *venir* + *de* + infinitive for "just now":
> *Je viens de manger* — I just ate
> *Elle vient de partir* — She just left

Use imperfect of *venir* for "had just" (in past narrative):
> *Il venait d'éteindre la lumière* — He had just turned off the light
> *Elle venait d'arriver* — She had just arrived

### 45. The pluperfect (plus-que-parfait) — "had done"
Imperfect of auxiliary + past participle. One step further back in the past.
> *La nuit **était tombée*** — Night had fallen
> *Il **avait compris*** — He had understood
> *Elle **s'était levée*** — She had got up

### 46. mal + past participle as adjective
> *mal éclairé* — poorly lit
> *mal compris* — misunderstood
> *mal traduit* — badly translated
Opposite with *bien*: *bien éclairé, bien compris, bien traduit*

### 47. entendre / voir / sentir + infinitive
Perception verbs followed directly by an infinitive:
> *il entendit frapper* — he heard a knock (heard knocking)
> *je la vis partir* — I saw her leave
> *il sentait venir l'orage* — he could feel the storm coming

### 48. faire + infinitive (the causative)
To have / make someone do something, or cause an action:
> *Il les fit entrer* — He showed them in / made them enter
> *Je fais réparer ma voiture* — I'm having my car repaired
> *Elle me fait rire* — She makes me laugh

### 49. Direct object pronouns — le / la / les
"Him / her / it / them" when they are the object, placed **before** the verb:
> *Je **le** vois* — I see him / it
> *Elle **les** connaît* — She knows them
> *Il **la** regardait* — He was watching her

### 50. Inversion in dialogue tags — dit-il / demanda-t-elle
After a quoted line, French inverts verb and subject with a hyphen:
> *« … », **dit-il*** — "…" he said
> *« … », **demanda-t-elle*** — "…" she asked
A **-t-** buffer is added when the verb ends in a vowel and the pronoun begins with one (*a-t-il*, *demanda-t-elle*, *viendra-t-on*).

### 51. d'une voix + adjectif (manner)
The standard French way to describe *how* someone speaks. The adjective agrees feminine with *voix*:
> *d'une voix **calme*** — in a calm voice
> *d'une voix **tremblante*** — in a trembling voice
> *d'une voix **basse*** — in a low voice
> *d'une voix **posée*** — in a measured voice

### 52. se laisser + infinitive — "to let oneself …"
Subject passively allows an action to happen to them:
> *se laisser tomber* — to sink / drop into
> *se laisser aller* — to let oneself go
> *se laisser convaincre* — to let oneself be convinced
> *se laisser porter* — to let oneself be carried

### 53. Superlative — le / la / les plus + adjectif
"The most …" / "the -est":
> *le fauteuil **le plus proche*** — the nearest armchair
> *la **plus belle*** — the most beautiful (fem.)
> *les **plus fort(e)s*** — the strongest
The article (*le / la / les*) agrees with the noun. For "the least," replace *plus* with *moins*: *la moins chère* (the least expensive).

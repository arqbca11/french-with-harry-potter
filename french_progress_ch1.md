## 🧱 Grammar

### 1. Gendered Nouns
Every noun is masculine or feminine. Always learn the gender with the word.
- **le / un** → masculine: *le bureau, un homme*
- **la / une** → feminine: *la chaise, une gorge*
- **les / des** → plural: *les flammes*

### 2. Negation — the Sandwich
Wrap the verb with **ne … pas**:
> *Je sais* → *Je **ne** sais **pas*** — I don't know

In casual spoken French, **ne** is often dropped: *"Je sais pas"*

### 3. Negation variants — all follow the sandwich pattern
| French | Meaning |
|---|---|
| *ne … pas* | not |
| *ne … plus* | no longer / not anymore |
| *ne … jamais* | never |
| *ne … rien* | nothing |
| *ne … personne* | no one |

### 4. The Imperfect Tense
Background descriptions, ongoing states, habitual past actions:
> *Il **était** seul* — He was alone (setting the mood)
> *Elle **avait** peur* — She was afraid (ongoing state)

### 5. Near Future — aller + infinitive
> *Je **vais** manger* — I'm going to eat
> *Il **va** partir* — He's going to leave

### 6. Object Pronouns Before the Verb
> *Il **le** regardait* — He was watching **him**
> *le nier* — to deny **it** (object goes before the infinitive too)

### 7. Reflexive Verbs (se + verb)
All reflexive verbs use **être** as auxiliary in passé composé.
> *se lever* — to get up · *se sentir* — to feel · *se rendre compte* — to realise

### 8. Possessives follow the noun's gender (not the owner's)
> *sa chaise* — his/her chair (*chaise* fem → **sa**)
> *son bureau* — his/her office (*bureau* masc → **son**)

### 9. il faut que + subjunctive — "must / have to"
Learn as a chunk:
> *Il faut que je parte* — I have to leave
> *Il faut que vous sachiez* — You must know

### 10. ce / ce … -là — "this" vs "that"
> *ce soir* — this evening · *ce soir-là* — that evening

### 11. Adjective Agreement — 4 forms
| | Masculine | Feminine |
|---|---|---|
| Singular | *vert* | *verte* |
| Plural | *verts* | *vertes* |
Same pattern: *fatigué/fatiguée*, *seul/seule*, *désolé/désolée*

### 12. avoir l'air + adjective — "to look / seem"
> *Il avait l'air fatigué* — He looked tired
> *Elle a l'air heureuse* — She looks happy

### 13. Dialogue inversion — dit-il / dit-elle
In written French, speech tags flip subject and verb:
> *"Je suis désolé," **dit-il*** — "I'm sorry," he said

### 14. de + infinitive after expressions of feeling
> *désolé **de** vous déranger* — sorry to disturb you
> *content **de** te voir* — happy to see you

### 15. avant de + infinitive
When the subject is the same in both clauses:
> *avant de parler* — before speaking

### 16. ce qui vs ce que
> *ce qui* = subject: *ce qui se passe* — what is happening
> *ce que* = object: *ce que vous faites* — what you are doing

### 17. il y a eu — "there was / there have been"
Passé composé of *il y a*:
> *Il y a eu des attaques* — There have been attacks

### 18. c'est + adjective
> *C'est vrai* · *C'est impossible* · *C'est difficile*

### 19. adjective + de + infinitive
> *impossible **de** le nier* — impossible to deny it
> *incapable **de** parler* — unable to speak

### 20. Negated passé composé
The *ne…pas* sandwich wraps the **auxiliary**, not the past participle:
> *ils **n'**ont **pas** réussi* — they did not manage

### 21. être vs avoir in passé composé
Use **être** for all reflexive verbs and these movement/state verbs:
*aller, venir, partir, arriver, naître, mourir, tomber, rester, monter, descendre*

### 22. réussir à + infinitive
The **à** is non-negotiable:
> *réussir **à** cacher* — manage to hide

### 23. responsable de (not pour)
> *responsable **de** tout cela* — responsible for all of this
Also: *content de, capable de, fier de, désolé de*

### 24. on as impersonal pronoun
*On* = they / someone / people (unspecified). Also replaces *nous* in spoken French:
> *on m'a demandé* — they asked me
> *on y va* — we're going

### 25. demander de + infinitive
> *on lui a demandé **d'**entrer* — they asked him to enter

### 26. indirect object pronoun lui
*Lui* = to him / to her — works for both genders, goes before the verb:
> *je lui ai dit* — I told him/her

### 27. adverbs in -ment
Feminine adjective + *-ment*:
| Adjective | Adverb |
|---|---|
| *brusque* | *brusquement* |
| *lent/lente* | *lentement* |
| *doux/douce* | *doucement* |
| *rapide* | *rapidement* |
| *vrai/vraie* | *vraiment* |

### 28. s'appeler — names
> *Je m'appelle…* — My name is…
> *Comment vous appelez-vous ?* — What is your name? (formal)

### 29. devoir — must / to have to
Essential modal verb:
> *je dois, tu dois, il doit, nous devons, vous devez, ils doivent*

### 30. avoir besoin de + noun/infinitive — "to need"
The *de* is non-negotiable:
> *nous avons besoin **de** temps* — we need time
> *j'ai besoin **de** dormir* — I need to sleep

### 31. bien que + subjunctive ⟨formal⟩
Concession connector — always triggers the subjunctive:
> *bien qu'il n'ait pas le choix* — even though he has no choice
TEF/TCF writing: signals sophistication.

### 32. The conditional tense (-rait / -raient)
Possibility, politeness, hypothetical situations:
> *pourraient être* — could be · *je voudrais* — I would like
Recognised by the **-rait / -raient** ending.

### 33. se rendre compte (de/que) — "to realise"
> *je me rends compte que* — I realise that
Reflexive → uses *être* in passé composé.

### 34. si + present → conditional
> *Si vous refusez* → *les conséquences pourraient être graves*
Never use future after *si* — a very common English-speaker mistake.

### 35. Article drops to de/d' after negation
> *une autre option* → *pas **d'**autre option*
> *des amis* → *je n'ai pas **d'**amis*

### 36. avoir à + infinitive — softer "to have to"
> *ce que vous avez **à** faire* — what you have to do

### 37. The imperative
Drop the subject, use the verb directly:
> *Faites !* — Do it! · *Partez !* — Leave! · *Venez !* — Come!
Note: *-s* drops from -er verbs in *tu* imperative: *Parle !* not *Parles !*

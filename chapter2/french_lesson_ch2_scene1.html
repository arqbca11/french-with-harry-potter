💡 venir de + infinitive = "to have just done something." This is one of the most useful structures in French. Je viens de manger = I just ate. Elle venait de partir = She had just left. It always uses the present tense of venir (for "just now") or imperfect (for "had just"). Compare with German gerade — same idea.

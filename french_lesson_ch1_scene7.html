💡 On is one of the most versatile pronouns in French. It means "they/someone/people" when you don't want to specify. On m'a dit que = I was told that. On parle français ici = French is spoken here. In spoken French, on also replaces nous constantly: on y va = we're going.

💡 s'appeler is the reflexive verb for names — essential in French. Je m'appelle… = My name is… Il s'appelle… = His name is… Comment vous appelez-vous ? = What is your name? (formal). You'll use this in every introduction.

💡 French adverbs in -ment are formed from the feminine adjective + -ment. brusque → brusquement. lent/lente → lentement (slowly). doux/douce → doucement (gently). It's the most systematic pattern in French — once you know it, you can form hundreds of adverbs instantly.
